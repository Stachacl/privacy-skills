# Governance Framework Builder

Build a lightweight privacy and AI governance framework for a team or organisation, covering policies, registers, roles, review processes, and incident response.

## When to use this

- Your team or organisation does not have a formal privacy or AI governance structure and needs one to start from.
- You want to move from ad hoc privacy decisions to a documented, repeatable process.
- You need a practical framework that staff can actually use without legal training.

## The checklist

1. Assess what is already in place: policies, registers, roles, and processes.
2. Draft a data handling policy covering what is collected, how it is used, and how long it is kept.
3. Draft an AI use policy covering approved tools, restricted uses, and prohibited uses.
4. Create a data assets register listing what personal data the organisation holds and where.
5. Create an AI tools register with approval status for each tool.
6. Define roles: who is responsible for privacy decisions, data subject requests, and incident response.
7. Document processes for handling data subject access requests, privacy incidents, and new tool onboarding.
8. Define a review schedule for policies and registers.
9. Confirm who approves changes to the framework.

## Examples

- A 20-person product company builds a lightweight governance pack covering their AI tools register, a one-page AI use policy, and a data handling summary.
- A consultancy creates a clear role assignment so that privacy decisions do not stall when the usual contact is unavailable.
- A team formalises their informal practices into a written policy before a client audit requires it.
