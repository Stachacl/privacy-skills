# Safe Sharing Version

Rewrite messages, documents, examples, screenshots, or case studies into safer sharing versions by removing or generalising sensitive details.

## When to use this

- You want to share something externally and need to check whether it contains details that should be removed first.
- You are preparing content for LinkedIn, a website, a report, or a support ticket and want a safer version.
- You have internal content that is useful as an example but contains names, client details, or confidential information.

## The checklist

1. Identify the destination: LinkedIn, vendor email, support ticket, public post, report, or documentation.
2. Check for real names of individuals.
3. Check for company or client names that have not been cleared for publication.
4. Check for exact locations, dates, or identifying combinations of details.
5. Check for customer details, account numbers, or internal system information.
6. Check for financial details, contract values, or pricing.
7. Check for confidential business strategy or unreleased product information.
8. Decide what to remove, generalise, replace with synthetic examples, or aggregate.
9. Produce a rewritten version and note what was changed.

## Examples

- An internal project write-up is prepared for a blog post. Client names are replaced with sector descriptions and financial figures are removed.
- A support ticket is prepared for a vendor. Customer names and account numbers are replaced with placeholder values.
- A LinkedIn post about a workplace lesson is reviewed and a colleague's name is replaced with their role.
