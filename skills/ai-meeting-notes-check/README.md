# AI Meeting Notes Check

Review AI meeting notes, transcripts, summaries, and recordings for privacy and confidentiality risks before storing or sharing them.

## When to use this

- You are using an AI note-taker or transcription bot in meetings with clients, staff, or partners.
- You want to check whether meeting recordings and summaries are stored and shared safely.
- You are deciding whether to allow AI tools like Otter, Fireflies, or Copilot into sensitive meetings.

## The checklist

1. Check whether all participants know an AI tool is recording or transcribing the meeting.
2. Identify whether sensitive topics are likely to be discussed (HR matters, health, finances, client information).
3. Check where the tool stores recordings, transcripts, and summaries.
4. Check whether the tool uses meeting content for model training.
5. Check whether this is an approved enterprise tool or a consumer service.
6. Review who can access the stored notes and for how long they are kept.
7. Consider whether written meeting minutes would be a safer alternative.

## Examples

- An AI note-taker joins a client call and stores a transcript in a US cloud service. The client was not informed.
- Meeting summaries containing employee performance details are stored in a shared team folder accessible to the whole company.
- A freelance facilitator uses a consumer transcription app for a strategy workshop without disclosing it to attendees.
