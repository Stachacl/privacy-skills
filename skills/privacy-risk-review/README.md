# Privacy Risk Review

Identify and rate privacy risks in a project, process, system, or planned change before making a decision or launching something new.

## When to use this

- You are assessing privacy risks before launching a product, onboarding a vendor, or making a change that involves personal data.
- You need a structured way to identify and rate risks for a project review or team discussion.
- You want a lightweight alternative to a full formal assessment for lower-stakes decisions.

## The checklist

1. Describe the project, system, or change being reviewed.
2. Identify what personal or sensitive data is involved.
3. Check whether personal data could be accessed by unauthorised people.
4. Check whether data could be shared with the wrong person or system through a design flaw.
5. Check whether more data is being collected or retained than is needed.
6. Check whether people know their data is being collected and whether there is a lawful basis for it.
7. Check whether personal data is shared with vendors, partners, or AI tools.
8. Check whether AI or automation is involved in processing or making decisions about personal data.
9. Check what would happen if this data were lost, stolen, or leaked.
10. Rate each risk: low, medium, or high.
11. Recommend mitigations for each risk.

## Examples

- A risk review before a product launch identifies that a vendor integration does not have a data processing agreement in place.
- A review of a new automated decision system reveals that decisions are made using data the people affected cannot access or correct.
- A risk review before migrating to a new cloud platform identifies that some datasets are retained in the old system after migration.
