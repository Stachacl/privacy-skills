# LinkedIn Post Privacy Check

Review LinkedIn posts for accidental privacy, confidentiality, customer, workplace, or personal data exposure before publishing.

## When to use this

- You are about to publish a post that includes workplace stories, lessons learned, or case study details.
- You want to check a screenshot or image before including it in a professional post.
- You are sharing a customer success story and want to confirm the details are safe to publish.

## The checklist

1. Check whether the post includes a customer or client name without confirmed permission to publish.
2. Check whether the post includes information about a specific person (colleague, client, candidate) that they have not consented to share publicly.
3. Check whether any screenshots show names, account details, internal interfaces, or confidential data.
4. Check whether the post includes financial details, contract values, or pricing that should remain internal.
5. Check whether the post includes details that could identify an individual or organisation even without naming them.
6. Check whether the post includes internal system names, tool names, or architecture details.
7. Check whether the post mentions security incidents or vulnerabilities in more detail than is appropriate.
8. Consider generalising: "a mid-sized logistics company" instead of the company name, "a lead developer" instead of a name.

## Examples

- A team leader posts a "proud of this result" story that includes enough specific detail about a client project to identify the company without naming them.
- A developer shares a screenshot of a dashboard to illustrate a point, and user email addresses are visible in the table.
- A recruiter posts about a candidate's interview experience, including details that could identify the candidate even without using their name.
