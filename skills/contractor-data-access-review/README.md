# Contractor Data Access Review

Review contractor, freelancer, consultant, or external partner access to sensitive data, systems, documents, or AI tools before granting it.

## When to use this

- You are about to give a contractor or freelancer access to internal systems or documents.
- You want to review what a current external partner can access and whether it is still appropriate.
- You need to check whether contractors are using AI tools with your data without authorisation.

## The checklist

1. Identify what data, systems, or documents the contractor needs access to.
2. Confirm that access is limited to what is needed for their role, not broader than necessary.
3. Check whether the access is time-limited or will need to be revoked manually.
4. Check whether the contractor is subject to a confidentiality agreement or NDA covering the data they access.
5. Identify whether the contractor handles personal data covered by data protection law, and whether a data processing agreement is in place.
6. Check whether the contractor is using AI tools with your data (including uploading documents to consumer AI services).
7. Confirm that access can be revoked promptly when the engagement ends.
8. Review access permissions for any previous contractors who may still have active accounts.

## Examples

- A freelance copywriter is given edit access to a Google Drive folder containing unreleased product strategy documents far beyond their scope.
- A contractor uses a personal AI writing tool to draft content and uploads internal brand documents to it without approval.
- A previous contractor's login credentials were never deactivated and their account still has access to a CRM with customer records.
