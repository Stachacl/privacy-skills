# Sensitive Document Workflow Review

Trace how sensitive documents move through a workflow to identify risks in storage, access, sharing, and disposal.

## When to use this

- You are reviewing how your team handles contracts, HR files, financial records, legal documents, or health records.
- You want to check whether sensitive documents are shared, stored, or disposed of safely.
- You are preparing for an audit or reviewing document handling practices before a policy update.

## The checklist

1. Identify the type of document and its sensitivity level.
2. Check where documents are created: locally, in cloud tools, via email, or through AI tools.
3. Check where documents are stored: shared drive, cloud storage, email, local disk.
4. Check who has access to the storage location and whether access permissions are set correctly.
5. Check how documents are shared: email, link, portal, or USB.
6. Check whether shared links are restricted to specific people or are open to anyone with the link.
7. Check whether documents are sent to external parties: vendors, consultants, clients.
8. Check whether documents are uploaded to AI tools (ChatGPT, Copilot, Claude) or third-party SaaS tools.
9. Check how documents are deleted when no longer needed.
10. Check whether old versions are retained unnecessarily.

## Examples

- A legal team stores signed contracts in a shared folder that all staff can access, including contractors.
- An HR manager uploads a disciplinary letter to a consumer AI tool to check the tone before sending.
- Financial reports are emailed to a consultant using a general company email address that multiple people have access to.
