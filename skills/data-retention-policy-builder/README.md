# Data Retention Policy Builder

Create practical data retention rules for documents, forms, recordings, logs, exports, and datasets so your team knows how long to keep information and when to delete it.

## When to use this

- You need to define how long different types of data should be kept before deletion or archiving.
- You are reviewing your current retention practices and want to set clearer rules.
- You are preparing a data retention policy for staff, auditors, or a vendor questionnaire.

## The checklist

1. List the data types your organisation holds: documents, emails, recordings, logs, exports, CRM records, form submissions.
2. For each data type, identify the purpose for which it is held and how long that purpose requires it to be kept.
3. Check whether any legal, regulatory, or contractual obligations require a minimum or maximum retention period.
4. Define who is responsible for reviewing and deleting each data type.
5. Check whether deletion is automatic or manual, and whether the process actually runs.
6. Check whether deleted data is also removed from backups, archives, and third-party systems.
7. Check whether meeting recordings, AI transcripts, and chat logs are covered by the retention policy.
8. Define what happens at the end of a retention period: deletion, anonymisation, or archiving.

## Examples

- Staff recordings from HR meetings are stored indefinitely because no one defined a retention period for them.
- Customer form submissions are kept for five years in a production database because "it might be useful" even though the retention policy says two years.
- A log retention policy says 30 days but the logging service is retaining 90 days by default because no one changed the configuration.
