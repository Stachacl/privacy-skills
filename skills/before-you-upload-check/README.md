# Before You Upload Check

Review a document, image, message, or dataset before uploading, pasting, or sharing it with an external service to catch privacy risks early.

## When to use this

- You are about to upload a file to a vendor portal, support ticket, or cloud tool and want a quick check.
- You need to share a document externally and want to confirm it is safe to send as-is.
- You are not sure whether the content you are about to upload contains personal or sensitive information.

## The checklist

1. Identify the destination: AI tool, vendor portal, email, support ticket, cloud storage, or public post.
2. Check for names, email addresses, phone numbers, physical addresses, or IDs.
3. Check for customer or client information.
4. Check for employee or HR information.
5. Check for financial details, contracts, or pricing.
6. Check for health, legal, or other sensitive categories.
7. Check for internal system names, credentials, or architecture details.
8. Classify the risk: low, medium, or high.
9. Consider whether a redacted, generalised, or excerpt version can be shared instead.

## Examples

- A support agent uploads a customer invoice PDF to a third-party tool to extract a table. The invoice includes the customer's home address and tax ID.
- A developer pastes a YAML config file into an AI coding assistant that includes a production database connection string.
- A team member uploads a meeting transcript to a cloud transcription service without removing the names of employees discussed.
