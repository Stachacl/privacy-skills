# AI Tool Onboarding Privacy Check

Review a new AI tool before your team adopts it, covering approved uses, data restrictions, vendor terms, logging, retention, training use, and staff guidance.

## When to use this

- A team member has requested a new AI service and you need to assess it before approving use.
- You are trialling a new AI tool and want to understand the data risks before rolling it out.
- You are building an approved AI tools register and need a consistent way to evaluate each tool.

## The checklist

1. Identify what the tool will be used for and which teams will use it.
2. Identify what types of data staff are likely to share with the tool (documents, prompts, customer data).
3. Check whether the tool is a consumer product or an enterprise service with data protection terms.
4. Check whether prompts and inputs are logged by the vendor.
5. Check whether the vendor uses customer data to train its models.
6. Check where data is stored and in which region.
7. Check whether a data processing agreement (DPA) is available.
8. Define what data staff are and are not allowed to share with the tool.
9. Confirm who is responsible for reviewing the tool and when the review will be repeated.

## Examples

- A marketing team wants to use an AI image generator. On review, the free tier retains all uploaded images and uses them for model training.
- A support team wants to use an AI summarisation tool with customer tickets. The enterprise plan excludes training use but the free plan does not.
- A developer adds an AI coding assistant without checking whether code and comments are logged by the vendor.
