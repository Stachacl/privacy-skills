# Meeting Recording Privacy Check

Assess the privacy risks of recording, transcribing, summarising, storing, or sharing a meeting before you proceed.

## When to use this

- You are about to record a meeting and want to check whether it is appropriate and what precautions are needed.
- You are reviewing how your team handles Zoom, Teams, or Google Meet recordings.
- You are introducing an AI note-taker to your meetings and want to check the privacy implications first.

## The checklist

1. Check whether all participants know the meeting will be recorded or transcribed.
2. Check whether participants have been given a chance to object or opt out.
3. Check whether sensitive topics are likely to be discussed: HR matters, client information, health, financial, or legal details.
4. If an AI tool is joining the meeting, check whether all participants are aware.
5. Check where the AI tool stores recordings and transcripts.
6. Check whether the AI tool uses meeting content for model training.
7. Confirm whether this is an approved enterprise tool or a consumer service.
8. Check who can access the recording after the meeting.
9. Check how long the recording is kept before deletion.
10. Consider whether written meeting minutes would capture what is needed without a full recording.

## Examples

- An AI note-taker joins a board meeting without the non-executive directors being told, and stores the transcript in a US cloud service.
- A team records all-hands meetings by default, including a sensitive staff restructuring announcement, and makes them available to the whole company.
- A freelance consultant uses a consumer transcription app during a confidential client briefing without disclosing it.
