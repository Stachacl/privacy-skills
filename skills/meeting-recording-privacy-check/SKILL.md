# Meeting Recording Privacy Check

## Purpose

Review whether it is appropriate to record, transcribe, summarise, or store a meeting, and what precautions are needed.

## Thinking style

Act as a plain-language privacy advisor.

Focus on:

- whether consent was genuinely given or assumed
- what sensitive content was likely captured and whether it should be stored
- whether a recording is actually necessary or whether notes would be safer

## Discovery questions

Ask:

1. Who was in the meeting — internal staff only, or external people (clients, candidates, students)?
2. Did everyone know in advance that the meeting would be recorded or transcribed?
3. What was discussed — were HR, client, legal, health, or sensitive matters covered?
4. Where is the recording stored and who can access it?
5. Is an AI note-taking tool involved — which one, and is it an approved enterprise tool?

## Workflow

1. Identify who is in the meeting and what will be discussed.
2. Check whether recording consent is in place.
3. Identify sensitive information likely to be discussed.
4. Review how recordings, transcripts, and summaries will be stored, accessed, and deleted.
5. Consider whether meeting minutes are a safer alternative.

## Checklist

**Consent and notice:**

- [ ] Do all participants know the meeting will be recorded?
- [ ] Have they been given a chance to object or opt out?
- [ ] Is consent required in this context (e.g. legal, HR, school, client)?

**What may be discussed:**

- [ ] Personal information about individuals?
- [ ] Customer or client information?
- [ ] Employee or HR matters?
- [ ] Student or child information?
- [ ] Health, financial, or legal information?
- [ ] Confidential business strategy?

**AI transcription and note-taking tools:**

- [ ] Is an AI note-taker joining the meeting (e.g. Otter.ai, Fireflies, Copilot)?
- [ ] Do all participants know an AI tool is present?
- [ ] Where does the AI tool store recordings and transcripts?
- [ ] Does the AI tool use meeting content for model training?
- [ ] Is this an approved enterprise tool or a consumer service?

**Storage and access:**

- [ ] Where is the recording stored?
- [ ] Who can access it?
- [ ] Is access limited to people who need it?
- [ ] How long is it kept before deletion?

**Safer alternatives:**

- [ ] Would written meeting minutes capture what is needed without a full recording?
- [ ] Could a summary be shared instead of the full recording?

## Output format

Return:

- Privacy risk level: low, medium, or high
- Key risks identified
- Changes recommended before recording
- Storage and access recommendations
- Whether minutes are a safer option in this case
- Open questions

## Anti-patterns

Avoid:

- assuming consent because no one objected when the recording started
- treating AI-generated summaries as less sensitive than full recordings
- ignoring jurisdiction — recording consent rules vary significantly by country
- recommending meeting minutes as a safer alternative without considering whether they capture what is needed
- assuming a tool is safe because it is an enterprise product without checking training and storage terms

## Disclaimer

This is a practical review tool, not legal advice, compliance certification, or a formal consent framework.
