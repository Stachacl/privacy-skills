# Staff AI Training Checklist

Create practical staff training checklists for safe AI use, data handling, approved tools, restricted information, review steps, and escalation.

## When to use this

- You are preparing AI awareness training for staff and want a structured checklist to base it on.
- You want to check whether your existing training covers the key risks of AI use at work.
- You are onboarding new staff and want to give them clear guidance on safe AI use from day one.

## The checklist

1. Confirm staff know which AI tools are approved for use and which are not.
2. Confirm staff know what types of data must not be uploaded or pasted into AI tools.
3. Confirm staff know the difference between consumer AI tools and enterprise tools with data protection terms.
4. Check that staff know how to report an AI-related privacy concern or incident.
5. Check that staff understand that AI outputs may be inaccurate and should be reviewed before use.
6. Check that training covers document upload risks: contracts, HR files, customer data, financial records.
7. Check that training covers prompt writing: what to avoid including in prompts.
8. Check that training covers meeting and recording risks: AI note-takers, transcription tools.
9. Check that training is updated when new tools are approved or when the policy changes.
10. Confirm staff know where to find the AI use policy and the approved tools register.

## Examples

- A new employee onboarding checklist includes a section on approved AI tools with three rules: no customer data, no credentials, no confidential strategy.
- Annual training is refreshed to include guidance on AI note-takers after the company adopts a meeting transcription tool.
- A team manager spots a gap in training: staff are not aware that the free tier of a writing tool logs all prompts.
