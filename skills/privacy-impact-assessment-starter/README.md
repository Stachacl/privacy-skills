# Privacy Impact Assessment Starter

Create a lightweight privacy impact assessment starter for a project, workflow, tool, or system to capture key risks before launch or change.

## When to use this

- Your team is about to launch a new product, process, or tool that involves personal data and needs a documented early review.
- You need a structured starting point for a privacy impact assessment without a full compliance programme.
- You are preparing a project and want a consistent way to capture and communicate privacy risks.

## The checklist

1. Describe the project, tool, or process being assessed and its purpose.
2. Identify what personal or sensitive data is involved.
3. Document where the data comes from and how it is collected.
4. Map where data is stored, who can access it, and how long it is kept.
5. Identify where data is shared: internally, with vendors, or with AI tools.
6. Identify the key risks: unnecessary collection, weak access controls, logging, retention gaps, third-party exposure.
7. Rate each risk as low, medium, or high.
8. Document what mitigations are in place or planned.
9. Note any open questions that require legal, compliance, or specialist review.
10. Record who approved the assessment and when it should be reviewed again.

## Examples

- A team launching a customer feedback tool completes a starter assessment and discovers they had not considered how long survey responses would be retained.
- A privacy assessment for a new HR analytics tool identifies that the vendor's free tier uses data for model training, prompting a switch to the enterprise plan.
- An early assessment for a new internal AI assistant reveals that the system indexes all company documents without permission filtering, leading to a design change before build.
