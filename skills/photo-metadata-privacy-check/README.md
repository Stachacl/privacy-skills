# Photo Metadata Privacy Check

Review photos and images for hidden privacy risks, including embedded metadata, location data, visible faces, documents, screens, and identifying details, before posting or sharing them.

## When to use this

- You are about to post a photo on social media, a website, or a public document and want to check it first.
- You want to review photos before sharing them with a vendor, in a report, or in marketing materials.
- You are checking whether photos taken at a workplace event or meeting are safe to publish.

## The checklist

1. Check whether the image contains visible names, email addresses, phone numbers, or other contact details.
2. Check whether EXIF metadata is embedded in the file (GPS location, device ID, timestamp).
3. Check whether the location where the photo was taken could be identified from background details.
4. Check whether identifiable faces appear and whether the people involved have consented to the image being shared.
5. Check whether screens, documents, whiteboards, or notepads are visible with readable content.
6. Check whether uniforms, badges, lanyards, or ID cards are visible.
7. Check whether the image reveals internal infrastructure, security arrangements, or office layout.
8. Check whether children appear in the image.
9. Consider removing EXIF metadata before sharing using image editing tools or metadata stripping tools.

## Examples

- A team photo taken at a conference reveals ID badges with full names and job titles worn by people who have not consented to public sharing.
- A blog post photo of a team working at laptops shows a customer database visible on a screen in the background.
- A phone photo shared in a support ticket retains full GPS coordinates revealing the user's home address in the EXIF data.
