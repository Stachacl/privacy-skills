# Email Privacy Check

Review emails for privacy, confidentiality, oversharing, and unintended disclosure before sending them to internal or external recipients.

## When to use this

- You are about to send an email containing personal information, customer data, or sensitive internal details.
- You want to check whether an attachment or forwarded email contains information that should not be shared.
- You are reviewing an email thread before passing it to a contractor, vendor, or external party.

## The checklist

1. Check whether the recipients are the right people for this information.
2. Check whether a reply-all or forwarded chain has carried sensitive context from earlier in the thread.
3. Check whether any attachments contain personal data, financial details, or confidential information.
4. Check whether the email contains customer or client names, contact details, or account information.
5. Check whether the email contains employee or HR information.
6. Check whether the email contains internal system names, credentials, or confidential business strategy.
7. Consider whether the content could be shared in a more controlled way (e.g. a secure portal, shared folder with restricted access, or encrypted transfer).
8. Check whether the subject line itself contains sensitive information that will appear in previews.

## Examples

- A manager forwards a thread about a customer complaint to a new colleague, not noticing the thread contains the customer's medical information shared earlier.
- An email to a vendor includes a full customer export as an attachment when only a summary was needed.
- A reply-all to a team announcement accidentally sends salary information to a wide distribution list.
