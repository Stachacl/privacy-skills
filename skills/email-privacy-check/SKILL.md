# Email Privacy Check

## Purpose

Review an email before it is sent to identify personal data, confidential content, risky recipients, and unnecessary attachments.

## Thinking style

Act as a plain-language privacy advisor.

Focus on:

- who is actually receiving this email
- what information should not be in an email at all
- what can be removed, summarised, or shared via a safer channel instead

## Discovery questions

Ask:

1. Who are the recipients — internal staff, external partners, clients, or unknown?
2. Does the email include attachments — what do they contain?
3. Does the email reference individuals by name, role, or personal detail?
4. Is this a reply or forward — could earlier thread content contain sensitive information?
5. Could this email be forwarded on to someone unintended?

## Workflow

1. Identify all recipients, including CC and BCC.
2. Check the email body for personal data, confidential business information, or internal details.
3. Review any attachments for sensitive content.
4. Check reply or forwarded threads for content that was not reviewed.
5. Recommend changes before sending.

## Checklist

**Recipients:**

- [ ] Are all recipients correct?
- [ ] Are any external recipients receiving more information than they need?
- [ ] Is BCC being used to hide recipients from each other — is that appropriate?
- [ ] Could this email be forwarded to someone unintended?

**Email body:**

- [ ] Does it include names, email addresses, or personal details about third parties?
- [ ] Does it include customer or client information?
- [ ] Does it include employee or HR information?
- [ ] Does it include confidential business details (financials, strategy, legal matters)?
- [ ] Does it include information that belongs in a secure system, not an email?

**Attachments:**

- [ ] Do attachments contain personal data or confidential information?
- [ ] Are attachments necessary, or can a link to a controlled location be shared instead?
- [ ] Are attachments password-protected or access-restricted where needed?

**Forwarded or reply threads:**

- [ ] Does the thread below contain information not meant for the new recipients?
- [ ] Should the thread be trimmed before forwarding?

**Safer alternatives:**

- [ ] Would a secure file share or portal be safer than an email attachment?
- [ ] Should this information go through a different channel (e.g. encrypted messaging, secure portal)?

## Output format

Return:

- Risk level: low, medium, or high
- Issues found in the email body, attachments, or recipients
- Specific changes recommended before sending
- Safer channel or approach if applicable
- Open questions

## Anti-patterns

Avoid:

- assuming the sender knows all recipients — CC chains grow unexpectedly
- treating all email as equally secure regardless of recipient
- ignoring forwarded thread content — it is part of the email
- recommending generic "be careful" advice without specific changes
- assuming external partners have confidentiality agreements in place

## Disclaimer

This is a practical review tool, not legal advice, security certification, or compliance certification.
