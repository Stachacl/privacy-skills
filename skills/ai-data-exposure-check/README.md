# AI Data Exposure Check

Review how AI tools are being used across a team or organisation to find where personal or sensitive data may be exposed, stored, logged, or shared unintentionally.

## When to use this

- Your team uses AI tools and you want to check what data is being shared with them.
- You are onboarding a new AI service and need to understand the data risks.
- You have noticed staff pasting customer or internal data into consumer AI tools.

## The checklist

1. List all AI tools in use, including consumer tools like free ChatGPT or Gemini.
2. For each tool, identify what data is shared via prompts, uploads, or integrations.
3. Check whether prompts or responses are logged by the provider or used for model training.
4. Check whether any AI integrations have access to email, documents, or internal systems.
5. Check whether staff know which tools are approved and what must not be shared.
6. Check whether API keys or credentials are ever included in prompts.
7. Identify gaps and recommend safer practices.

## Examples

- A team is using a free AI writing tool to draft customer emails. The prompts include real customer names and complaint details.
- An AI browser extension has been granted access to all pages, including internal admin tools.
- Staff are uploading HR documents to a consumer AI service to get summaries, not realising the content may be logged.
