# Minimum Data Needed

Decide the minimum amount of personal, sensitive, or business data needed for a form, workflow, decision, or service, and identify what can be removed or reduced.

## When to use this

- You are reviewing a form, dataset, or process and want to reduce how much personal data you collect.
- You want to apply a "minimum necessary" principle to a workflow before launching it.
- You are simplifying a data model and need to decide which fields can be dropped.

## The checklist

1. List every data field or data point being collected.
2. For each field, ask: why is this needed, and what is the consequence of not collecting it?
3. Check whether a less specific version could be used (e.g. age range instead of date of birth).
4. Check whether the field could be optional rather than required.
5. Check whether free-text fields might invite people to share more than you need.
6. Check whether data is stored after its purpose has been served.
7. Check who can access this data once it has been collected.
8. Check whether any of this data is shared with third parties or analytics services.

## Examples

- A booking form asks for a full date of birth when only "are you over 18?" is needed.
- A contact form asks for job title, company name, phone number, and address when only a name and email are needed to respond.
- An internal tool stores full user profiles for reporting purposes when only aggregate counts are needed.
