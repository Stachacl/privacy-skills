# Safe AI Use Policy Builder

Create a short, clear AI use policy for staff that covers approved tools, restricted data, safe prompting, document uploads, review steps, and escalation.

## When to use this

- Your organisation needs a practical AI use policy that staff will actually read and follow.
- You want to move from informal guidance to a written policy without creating a lengthy compliance document.
- You are updating an existing policy to reflect the AI tools your team now uses.

## The checklist

1. List the AI tools staff are currently using or requesting approval for.
2. Identify what types of data the organisation handles (customer data, HR records, financial information, health data).
3. Draft a purpose statement explaining what the policy is for and who it applies to.
4. Define approved uses: drafting, summarising, coding assistance, ideation.
5. Define restricted uses that require approval: customer-facing AI outputs, use with sensitive documents.
6. Define prohibited uses: pasting customer PII into consumer AI tools, sharing credentials or confidential business strategy.
7. List specific data types staff must not upload or paste: customer names and emails, financial records, health information, HR records, credentials.
8. Define the review and approval process for new AI tools.
9. Define the escalation process if data may have been shared incorrectly.
10. Write a short plain-language summary (3–5 bullet points) for staff to remember.

## Examples

- A 15-person agency creates a one-page AI policy covering their approved tools, a list of what must not be uploaded, and a short staff summary.
- A healthcare organisation adds a specific rule prohibiting the use of patient data with any AI tool that is not on the approved enterprise list.
- A product team updates their policy to include the AI coding assistant they recently adopted, with a note that credentials must never appear in prompts.
