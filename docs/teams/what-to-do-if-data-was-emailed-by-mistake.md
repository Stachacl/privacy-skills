# What to do if data was emailed by mistake

## Short answer

Act quickly, stay calm, and document what happened. The first steps are to contain what you can, understand what was involved, and inform the right people internally. Whether external notification is required depends on what was in the email and who received it.

## What to check

1. Establish what was sent: what data was in the email or attachment?
2. Identify who received it: one wrong person, a small group, a large list?
3. Check whether the email can be recalled (Outlook and some email systems allow this, though it is not always reliable).
4. If the data included customer or employee personal information, identify how many people could be affected.
5. Check the type of data: names and emails, financial data, health data, HR records, credentials? More sensitive data means higher risk.
6. Contact the unintended recipient promptly, explain the situation, and ask them to delete the email and not forward it.
7. Create a written record of what happened: what was sent, to whom, when, what you have done so far.
8. Inform a manager or your data/privacy contact internally.
9. Check whether your organisation has an incident response process to follow.
10. Consider whether the incident meets the threshold for regulatory notification in your jurisdiction.

**Steps in order:**

1. Try to recall or delete the email if possible
2. Contact the wrong recipient and ask them to delete it
3. Document what happened
4. Inform the right people internally
5. Assess whether external notification is needed
6. Review how to prevent it happening again

This is a practical first-response guide, not legal advice, and not formal breach notification advice. Whether notification to regulators or affected individuals is required depends on the nature of the data and your jurisdiction. Seek legal or compliance advice for any serious data incident.
