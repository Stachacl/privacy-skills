# Should this meeting be recorded?

## Short answer

Not every meeting needs to be recorded. Before recording, check whether everyone knows and has had a chance to opt out, whether the meeting involves sensitive topics, and whether meeting minutes would serve the purpose just as well. A recording is harder to control than a written summary.

## What to check

1. Check whether all participants know the meeting will be recorded or transcribed.
2. Check whether participants have been given a chance to object or opt out.
3. Check whether sensitive topics are likely to come up: HR matters, individual performance, health, legal, financial, or client-confidential information.
4. If an AI note-taker is joining, check whether all participants know it is present.
5. Check where the recording or transcript will be stored after the meeting.
6. Check who will have access to the recording.
7. Check how long the recording will be kept before deletion.
8. Check whether the AI or transcription tool is an approved enterprise service or a consumer tool.
9. Consider whether written meeting minutes would capture the key decisions and actions without a full recording.

**Some situations that warrant extra care:**

- HR, performance, or disciplinary meetings
- Client or customer calls where confidentiality was expected
- Meetings involving health, legal, or financial discussions
- School or community meetings involving children or vulnerable individuals
- Strategy sessions involving unreleased information
