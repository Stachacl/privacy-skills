# What should I blur in a screenshot?

## Short answer

Blur or remove anything that could identify a person, expose account details, reveal internal systems, or show confidential data. When in doubt, blur it. It is easier to share a clean screenshot than to explain an accidental disclosure.

## What to check

1. Look for names, email addresses, phone numbers, or physical addresses visible anywhere on screen.
2. Look for profile photos or avatars of real people.
3. Look for account usernames, user IDs, or customer reference numbers.
4. Look for financial information: balances, card numbers, invoices, pricing.
5. Look for health or medical information.
6. Look for government or official ID numbers.
7. Look for internal system names, internal URLs, or environment details (staging, production, admin panel).
8. Look for database IDs, API keys, tokens, or credentials.
9. Look for other people's messages or conversations visible in the interface.
10. Look for browser tabs, history, or bookmarks visible in the background.
11. Look for timestamps or location data that could identify someone.

**What to do with each issue:**

- **Blur or mosaic** — names, contact details, photos, IDs, account numbers
- **Crop** — remove parts of the screen not needed for the screenshot
- **Replace with placeholder** — substitute example values (e.g. `user@example.com`, `Order #12345`)
- **Recreate with dummy data** — for documentation or public posts, recreate the screenshot with made-up data
