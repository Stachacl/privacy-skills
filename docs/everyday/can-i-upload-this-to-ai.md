# Can I upload this to an AI tool?

## Short answer

It depends on what is in the content and which AI tool you are using. If the content includes real names, customer information, employee details, financial records, health data, credentials, or confidential business information, do not upload it to a consumer AI tool. Either redact it first or use an approved enterprise or local tool.

## What to check

1. Identify which AI tool you are using: is it a consumer product (free tier), an enterprise plan with data protection terms, or a local/on-prem tool?
2. Check whether the content includes names, email addresses, phone numbers, or IDs.
3. Check whether it includes customer or client information.
4. Check whether it includes employee or HR information.
5. Check whether it includes financial details, contracts, or pricing.
6. Check whether it includes health, legal, or other sensitive categories.
7. Check whether it includes internal system names, credentials, or architecture details.
8. Consider whether the content can be redacted or generalised before uploading.
9. If you are unsure, check whether an approved enterprise or on-prem version of the tool is available.

**General guidance:**

- Consumer AI tools (free tier): avoid uploading anything with real personal data, customer information, or confidential content.
- Enterprise AI plans: check the vendor's data terms — many enterprise plans exclude training use, but confirm this before uploading sensitive content.
- Local or on-prem AI tools: generally safer for sensitive content, but still apply the same checklist.
